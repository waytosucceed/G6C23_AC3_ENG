Story reading and analysis

Part 1 of the Story

Part 1 of the story follows Yan, a young man living in the hills of Carberry, Canada. Yan spends his days exploring the wilderness, finding joy in nature and learning about various animals.

One day, he discovers a deer track, which sparks his determination to hunt a deer, despite locals saying there are none in the area.

Yan embarks on a persistent quest, spending days and nights in the hills, developing his skills as a tracker and runner.

The story captures Yan's growth, his deep connection with nature, and his unwavering determination as he pursues his goal through changing seasons and challenging conditions.

Story Start: The beginning of a story grabs the reader’s attention and sets the stage for what’s to come.

Introduction of the Main Character: The main character is usually introduced at the beginning of the story. We learn who they are and sometimes what they like or do.

Setting up the Scene: The scene is where and when the story takes place. The setting helps the reader picture the story in their mind.

Character Growth: Good stories show characters growing or changing. Even if they haven’t changed yet, there are often hints that the character will learn or grow by the end of the story. Character growth refers to the changes a character undergoes throughout the story, often as a result of their experiences and challenges. This growth can be emotional, intellectual, or physical.

Understanding Symbolism: Symbolism is when an object, character, or event represents a larger idea or concept. It adds deeper meaning to the story and helps convey themes.

Importance of the Setting: The setting plays a crucial role in shaping the story, influencing the character’s development and the plot’s progression. The wilderness represents both challenges and opportunities for growth.

Words that I learnt from the story

Imprint: A mark or impression made by pressure; a lasting effect or influence.
Sentence: The child's handprint left an imprint in the wet cement.

Prickling: A tingling or stinging sensation.
Sentence: She felt a prickling sensation on her arm after touching the leaves.

Hoofmark: A mark or impression made by the hoof (the hard part of the foot of horses and some other animals) of an animal.
Sentence: The hoofmark was clearly visible in the muddy ground.
